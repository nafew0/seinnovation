Eduroam at BdREN

eduroam (education roaming) is the secure, world-wide roaming access service
developed for the international research and education community. It allows
students, researchers and staff from participating institutions to obtain
Internet connectivity across their own campus and when visiting any other
participating institution, simply by opening their laptop — using the login
credentials issued by their home institution.

User credentials are never shared with the site being visited. Instead they are
forwarded to the user's home institution, where they are verified and validated,
through a network of RADIUS servers run by the institutions and the
participating National Research and Education Networks (NRENs). In Bangladesh,
BdREN operates National RADIUS Servers connected to upstream European and Asian
servers.

The service is available at thousands of hotspots in more than 100 territories
worldwide. Thanks to a global agreement, the eduroam Wi-Fi roaming service is
free of charge to users.

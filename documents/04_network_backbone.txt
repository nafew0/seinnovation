BdREN Network and Backbone

BdREN has taken a 20-year lease of more than 3,200 km of Optical Ground Wire
(OPGW) from the Power Grid Company of Bangladesh (PGCB), signed in 2011, to build
its national backbone. Every public university is provided with 1G campus
connectivity, and distribution points carry dual 10 Gbps links to the core
network.

The backbone connects 34 public universities, 2 international universities
(Islamic University of Technology and Asian University for Women), 3 private
universities (North South University, Independent University and BRAC
University) and the University Grants Commission.

For commercial Internet, BdREN has subscribed 4 Gbps of bandwidth from multiple
upstream providers; individual universities subscribe an allocation according to
their own requirements. International academic traffic is carried over dedicated
connectivity to the Trans-Eurasia Information Network (TEIN), letting researchers
and students collaborate with the global research and education community.

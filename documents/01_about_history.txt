About BdREN

BdREN is the national platform that links the country's universities and
research institutions to one another and to the global research and education
community. Its mission is to be an enabler for excellence in research and
education by strengthening the quality of research, education, innovation and
strategic collaboration at home and abroad.

The network was implemented by the University Grants Commission (UGC) of
Bangladesh on behalf of the Ministry of Education under the Higher Education
Quality Enhancement Project (HEQEP), through joint financing by the Government
of Bangladesh and the World Bank.

Bangladesh Research and Education Network (BdREN) is the only National Research
and Education Network (NREN) in Bangladesh. It began operating in 2009 under
HEQEP, when connectivity among the first institutions was established; this is
the year in which BdREN was established. The non-profit BdREN Trust was later
formally constituted in 2014 to operate and maintain the network on a
sustainable basis, with a board of trustees drawn from the universities, UGC and
the Ministry of Education.

A leased 3,200 km Optical Ground Wire (OPGW) backbone was rolled out from 2011
onwards, and the network had reached 34 universities by 2015. In 2025 BdREN
recorded a positive net surplus for the first time, demonstrating its financial
sustainability.
